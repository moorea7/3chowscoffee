About Three Chows Coffee Company

Small-batch coffee, crafted with care — inspired by our family, our city, and our three chow chows.

My Love of Coffee

My love of coffee started early. Growing up in England, that often meant instant coffee — it was simply what was available. But everything changed once I got to university. There, I discovered real coffee, and I dove headfirst into experimenting with every brewing method I could find.

I tried everything from hand-grinding beans to brewing in a classic moka pot, learning how each technique brought out different flavors and textures. Those experiments sparked a curiosity that stayed with me, laying the foundation for what would eventually become my passion for roasting.

Early Coffee Work

After university, one of my first jobs was teaching at a college. To help supplement my income, I also worked part-time in a small coffee shop that roasted its own beans.

That experience was eye-opening — it was the first time I saw coffee taken from green to roasted, and it gave me an inside look at the care and craft that goes into every batch. It was drilled into me that roasting isn’t just about applying heat — it’s about bringing out the unique character of each bean.

At the same time, I learned how to make great brewed coffee and espresso. My mentor insisted that I pull shot after shot — and taste every one — until the consistency was right. That discipline trained my palate to recognize balance, nuance, and quality in the cup.

Before I ever began roasting myself, Maria — then early in our relationship — took me to visit coffee farms in her home country of Indonesia. I was fascinated watching small farms pick, roast, and sell their coffee to both locals and tourists alike. Those early visits, and many return trips since, gave me a deep appreciation for coffee at its source and broadened my perspective on its global culture.

How I Started Roasting

My roasting journey began when I backed an Indiegogo campaign for the Roma Pro roaster. At first, I experimented with its automatic modes, but it didn’t take long before I wanted more control. I connected the Roma Pro to Artisan to log temperatures and track roast curves in real time.

When the Roma Pro went down for repairs, I picked up a Skywalker roaster, which runs on Hibean. That setup gave me even greater visibility and control, allowing me to fine-tune heat, airflow, and timing with precision.

Still to this day, I roast in small 500-gram batches, continuing to hone the craft with the same curiosity and care that first drew me in.

From Hobby to Craft

As I got deeper into roasting, “better than store” coffee quickly became the norm. Friends and family didn’t want to wait for gifts — they asked to buy my coffee directly. Their encouragement pushed me from hobbyist to small-batch roaster.

Along the way, Maria — my wife, a coffee lover from Indonesia, and chief brand ambassador — brought her business background into the picture. She championed Three Chows from the beginning: introducing me to Indonesia’s coffee culture, sharing beans with her fellow teachers and friends, and always reminding me about the sales and marketing side so this passion could grow.

The Name: Three Chows

When I met Maria, I quickly fell in love — not only with her, but also with her chow chows. Their loyal, dignified (and sometimes stubborn) personalities fit our home perfectly. Over the years we’ve had a few, but our current three — Cowboy, Bruno, and Riri — are ever-present. They’re usually right outside the roasting-room door; they know this is something I love to do and want to be there when it’s happening.

Naming the venture Three Chows Coffee Company felt natural — a tribute to the dogs that bring so much joy to our lives, and to the warmth and companionship coffee represents.

Roasting Philosophy & Mission

At Three Chows, I believe coffee is at its best when it expresses the unique character of each bean. Every roast is guided by both data and intuition — science and craft working together to highlight sweetness, balance, and the story of origin.

My mission is simple: to share fresh, small-batch coffee roasted with care. Coffee that connects people, sparks joy, and brings the same sense of comfort and companionship as the three chows who inspired its name.

Seattle Roots

We live in Seattle — a city known for coffee, beer, the outdoors, and dogs — the perfect home for Three Chows. The culture here celebrates craft, quality, and community, the same values at the heart of every bag I roast. Whether I’m dialing in a roast curve or heading outside with the dogs, Seattle provides the backdrop that inspires this journey.
